KAKI KING

Solo guitarist Kaki King brings a fusion of jazz, punk and folk to her shows, not to mention a rare percussive technique and the use of unique tunings. That earned her the first-ever female “Guitar God” from Rolling Stone.

SISTER ROSETTA THARPE

Sister Rosetta Tharpe was a gifted jazz guitarist and vocalist, and even though she spent years staking her claim in the gospel world, Tharpe never abandoned her jazz origins. A prodigy who mastered guitar by age six, Tharpe’s legacy packs heaps of genuine feeling and tight musicianship.

ANNIE CLARK

Annie Clark a.k.a St. Vincent is a madcap artist who twists and distorts sound, writing gorgeous incisive guitar riffs. St. Vincent’s records are both luscious and challenging, mixing gorgeous alluring melodies with harrowing guitar noise and disorientating electronics. Her live performances are explosive, as she offers a new and wholly 21st century take on what a guitarist can be.

NANCY WILSON

Nancy Wilson's sweeping guitar work helped define ‘70s and ‘80s classic rock. As songwriter and guitarist for Heart, she brought loud-and-proud arena rock songs and high-volume power ballads to a hungry rock ‘n’ roll public. Wilson’s playing presented acoustic fingerstyle work in an electric environment, and it came together with her sister Ann’s muscular vocals.

GABRIELA QUINTERO

Gabriela Quintero’s work as rhythm guitarist for Rodrigo y Gabriela defines the sound that has made the pair so popular. Her ferociously percussive strumming and virtuosic tapping helped the duo become the first ever instrumental acoustic rock act to storm the charts and pack concert halls.

JAN KUEHNEMUND

Jan Kuehnemund, the founding member and virtuoso lead guitarist for the all-female glam metal band Vixen started the band in 1973 when she was still in high school. Kicking open doors for women in the world of rock music, Jan crafted beautiful screaming guitar solos. The band became a regular on MTV and, in 1988, earned a gold record.
